The amazing story of Shavarsh Karapetyan

Soviet swimmer who saved 20 people from a sinking trolleybus

The story of saving people

On September 16, 1976, Shavarsh Karapetyan, his brother and a friend were going for a morning jog. By chance they found themselves at the site of a trolleybus crash into the Yerevan reservoir. Karapetyan began to rescue people from the water. He passed drowning people to his brother, and his brother transferred them to the boat, and from the boat to the car. At a depth of 10 meters with zero visibility, Shavarsh broke the rear window of the trolleybus with his feet and pulled out 46 passengers out of 92. 20 of them were brought back to life. The unconscious passengers were taken to the hospital. During one of his deep dives, Shavarsh realized that he had pulled out not a person, but a cushion from a trolleybus seat. According to him, he often dreamed about this pillow at night, as he could save another life.

Biography

  • 1953 - born in the city of Kirovakan (now Vanadzor), Armenia
  • 1975—1976 - served in one of the units of the Baku Air Defense District
  • 1985 - became a participant in extinguishing a fire at the Sports and Concert Complex in Yerevan, on Tsitsernakaberd Hill. Karapetyan was one of the first at the scene of the fire and began to help firefighters. During the operation to extinguish the building, he received injuries and burns, after which he spent a month in the hospital
  • 1991 - moved to Moscow
  • 2007 - the non-profit organization “Shavarsh Karapetyan Charitable Foundation” was registered, which carried out activities in the field of sports. Liquidated in 2021
  • 2013 - took part in the Olympic torch relay of the 2014 Sochi Games, held in Moscow. The fire of Shavarsh Karapetyan, who was running along Red Square, went out and was lit by an FSO officer
  • 2018 - was a confidant of the candidate for the position of President of the Russian Federation Vladimir Vladimirovich Putin
  • 2020 - was appointed to the post of Advisor to the Head of Bashkiria on a voluntary basis
